Rule 1 · Tone 3 + Tone 3 → Tone 2 + Tone 3
When two third-tone syllables collide, the first one rises to second tone.
nǐ hǎo→ sounds likení hǎo你好
shuǐ guǒ→shuí guǒ水果 · fruit
dǎ sǎo→dá sǎo打扫 · clean up
xǐ zǎo→xí zǎo洗澡 · take a shower
Rule 2 · Shortened finals
Some written finals are abbreviations — these are how they actually sound.
ui=u + eihui · shui · rui
iu=i + ouxiu · jiu · qiu
un=u + enkun · sun · cun · shun · chun · gun
üan=ü + ianxuan · quan · juan
üe=ü + iejue · nüe · que
ün=ü + inqun · xun · jun
Rule 3 · Hidden dots for ü (after j q x)
After j q x, the dots above ü are dropped — but it's still ü.
j q x + ü→ju · qu · xu
j q x + üe→jue · que · xue
j q x + üan→juan · quan · xuan
j q x + ün→jun · qun · xun
Rule 4 · Silent "y" + hidden dots ü
When ü starts a syllable, it's written with a silent y and the dots are dropped.
yu=ü
yue=üe
yun=ün
yuan=üan
Rule 5 · 不 sandhi
不 is tone 4 by default. Before another tone-4 syllable it shifts to tone 2.
tone 4 + tone 4 → tone 2 + tone 4
bú shì·不是
bú huì·不会
bú rè·不热
Rule 6 · Three pronunciations of 一
yī when 一 stands alone or ends a word.
xīng qī yī·星期一 · Monday
dì yī·第一 · the first
wǔ shí yī·五十一 · 51
yí before tone 4.
yí dìng·一定 · definitely
yí wàn·一万 · ten thousand
yí kuài·一块 · one piece
yì before tones 1, 2, or 3.
yì qǐ·一起 · together
yì diǎn·一点 · a little
yì tiān·一天 · one day
Rule 7 · z c s zh ch sh r + i (silent "i")
After these initials the "i" isn't pronounced like "ee" — it's a kind of held buzz on the consonant.
z c s r zh ch sh + i→zi · ci · si · ri · zhi · chi · shi
